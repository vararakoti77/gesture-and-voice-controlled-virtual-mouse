# How to Use the AI Virtual Mouse

## Getting Started

1. **Run the Program**  
   Double-click `AiVirtualMouse.exe`

2. **Allow Camera Access**  
   Your webcam will turn on - this is how the program sees your hand

3. **Position Your Hand**  
   Hold your hand in front of the camera, about 1-2 feet away. Make sure your whole hand is visible in the purple box shown on screen.

4. **Exit Anytime**  
   Press **Q** on your keyboard to close the program  
   Press **M** to keep/unkeep the camera window always on top

---

## Hand Gestures - What You Can Do

### 1. **Move the Mouse Cursor**
- **How:** Point with just your **index finger** (like pointing at something)
- **Keep other fingers down in a fist**
- Your finger movement controls where the cursor goes
- *Tip: Move slowly for better accuracy*

---

### 2. **Click (Pinch Method)**
- **How:** Touch your **thumb** and **index finger** together (like pinching)
- This is a regular left-click
- *Best for quick clicks*

---

### 3. **Click (Touch Method)**
- **How:** Put **index** and **middle finger** up, then bring them close together (almost touching)
- Also does a regular left-click
- *Good when you want more control*

---

### 4. **Select/Drag Things**
- **How:** Put **index** and **middle finger** up but keep them **apart** (not touching)
- This is like holding down the mouse button - use it to:
  - Select multiple files
  - Highlight text
  - Drag and drop items
- Move your hand while fingers are apart to drag

---

### 5. **Right-Click**
- **How:** Put up **three fingers** - index, middle, and ring finger
- Bring them close together
- Opens context menus (same as right-clicking with a mouse)

---

### 6. **Double-Click**
- **How:** Put up **thumb, index, and middle finger**, then bring them close together
- Opens folders and files

---

### 7. **Scroll Up and Down**
- **How:** Put up just your **index finger** and **pinky finger** (like a "rock on" sign)
- Move your hand **up** to scroll up
- Move your hand **down** to scroll down
- *Great for scrolling through websites or documents*

---

### 8. **Control Volume**
- **How:** Put up just your **thumb** and **pinky** (like making a phone gesture)
- Move them **closer together** = volume goes down
- Move them **further apart** = volume goes up
- You'll see a volume bar on screen

---

### 9. **Zoom In and Out**
- **How:** Put up **thumb** and **middle finger**
- Move them **apart** = zoom in
- Move them **closer** = zoom out
- *Works in browsers, maps, and most apps*

---

### 10. **Take a Screenshot**
- **How:** Show your **open palm** - all 5 fingers spread out
- Screenshot saves automatically to the program folder
- Filename will be like: `screenshot_1234567890.png`

---

### 11. **Play/Pause Media**
- **How:** Put up **4 fingers** - index, middle, ring, and pinky (thumb stays down)
- Works with music players, YouTube, videos, etc.

---

### 12. **Adjust Brightness** (Display Only)
- **How:** Put up **thumb** and **ring finger**
- Move them closer or apart
- *Note: This shows brightness percentage but may not actually change your screen brightness depending on your computer*

---

## Quick Tips

✋ **Keep your hand steady** - Quick movements can confuse the system

📐 **Stay in the purple box** - That's the active area where gestures work best

💡 **Good lighting helps** - The camera needs to see your hand clearly

⏱️ **Wait between gestures** - There's a short cooldown so you don't accidentally trigger things twice

🎯 **Practice makes perfect** - Try each gesture a few times to get the feel

---

## Troubleshooting

**Cursor is jumpy or too sensitive**
- Move your hand slower
- Keep your hand more steady
- Check if lighting is good

**Gestures not working**
- Make sure your whole hand is visible in the purple box
- Try doing the gesture a bit slower
- Check that you're using the right fingers

**Camera window disappeared**
- Press M to toggle it back on top
- Click on the window in your taskbar

**Program not responding**
- Close it (press Q or close the window)
- Run it again

## System Requirements

✅ Windows 10 or 11  
✅ Working webcam  
✅ No Python or special software needed  
✅ About 500MB free space

## Need Help?

If something isn't working:
1. Make sure your camera works (test it in another app)
2. Try running as Administrator
3. Check if Windows gave camera permission to the program

**Remember:** This is motion-controlled, so small movements go a long way. Start with simple gestures like moving the cursor and clicking, then try the advanced ones!
